# Welcome... to 142857.

*Ah, there you are. Come in, come in — do mind your step.*

You know, when I was a younger man, I ran a flea circus down in Petticoat Lane.
Little mechanical trapeze, a carousel, tiny tightrope — the children adored it.
But it was all an illusion, you see. Smoke and mirrors.

Here, my dear boy — here there are **no illusions**. What you are about to witness
is a genuine, living, breathing wonder of arithmetic. A number that **spins**.
A number that **rotates on itself** like a carousel made of digits. And unlike
my poor flea circus, I can assure you — *this one is real.*

I have **spared no expense.**

## The attraction

Her name is **142857**, and she is the most charming little number you'll ever meet.

Take her. Multiply her by two. Multiply her by three. By four, by five, by six.
Go on — don't be shy.

And what do you see? *The very same six digits.* Every time. Just starting
in a different place on the wheel. Like a little merry-go-round of ones and
fours and twos and eights and fives and sevens, forever turning.

And then — *then*, my dear — you multiply her by seven.

And she gives you **999999**.

Every. Single. Time.

> *"They're gonna have dinosaurs on their dinosaur tour?"*
>
> No, my boy. We're going to have **mathematics** on our mathematics tour.

## The tour

Welcome to the park. Mind the paddocks — though I promise you, nothing here bites.

- **Paddock One — The Trick.** Step right up. Press the buttons. Watch the ring
  turn. See her rotate with your own eyes.
- **Paddock Two — Why It Works.** Here we peek behind the curtain. The long
  division of 1 ÷ 7, the remainders chasing each other around the cycle, and
  a proof so clean you could eat your supper off it.
- **Paddock Three — How to Use It.** This is where you become the magician.
  Memorize one little number, and any seventh — `2/7`, `5/7`, the lot — comes
  pouring out of your head on command.
- **Paddock Four — The Divisibility Trick.** A companion act. Chop off the
  last digit, double it, take it away. Repeat. And like that — you know whether
  any number at all is divisible by seven. *Like that.*
- **Paddock Five — Fun Facts.** A gift shop, if you will. Curiosities for
  the road. Kaprekar's splits. The cousins at 1/17 and 1/19. The way she folds
  back on herself when you multiply by eight. Marvels, all of them.

## Visiting hours

She's a static creature — no feed required, no electric fences. A plain
browser will do nicely.

```bash
python3 -m http.server 8000
# then come visit her at http://localhost:8000
```

That's all. No dependencies. No build pipeline. No one in the control room
drinking shaving cream. Just open the door and walk in.

## The grounds

- `index.html` — the park itself
- `styles.css` — the landscaping
- `script.js` — the mechanisms, the rotating platforms, the little tricks that
  make her dance (uses `BigInt`, because one does not cut corners)
